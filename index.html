The Plan at a Glance

Two Hills, One Epic Loop

Munnar (south, near Kochi) & Wayanad (north, near Kozhikode) sit ~205 km apart. We enter via Kochi, explore Munnar's tea country, ride north to Wayanad, then loop home from Kozhikode — the cheapest, most scenic route with zero backtracking.

Train In & Out

Sabari Express to Kochi, return from Kozhikode — sleeper class, lowest fares.

Rented Bikes

2 two-wheelers for 4 — total freedom to chase viewpoints & save money.

Budget Homestays

Clean stays ~₹1,300/night for 4 — monsoon off-season rates.

Monsoon Magic

July = lush green, full waterfalls & the year's lowest prices.

July is peak monsoon. Expect heavy rain, fog & possible ghat-road closures in Munnar/Wayanad. Carry rain gear, ride slow, keep the plan flexible — but enjoy the lowest rates & greenest scenery of the year.

Travel Smart

Best Travel Tips & FAQ

Tap a question to expand.

Is July a good time to visit Munnar & Wayanad?

July is peak monsoon — lush greenery, full waterfalls and the lowest prices of the year. Downsides: heavy rain, fog, and occasional ghat-road/landslide closures. Great for budget + scenery if you stay flexible and check road status daily.

When should we book trains?

~60 days in advance on IRCTC to get confirmed sleeper berths at base fare. The return Kozhikode→Hyderabad train runs on select days — verify the exact date before locking your plan.

Do we need a licence for bike rental?

Yes — a valid driving licence is mandatory, plus a ₹1,000 deposit or ID. Helmets are required by law. Ride slowly on wet hairpin roads and avoid night riding.

How much cash should we carry?

Keep a good chunk in cash — many homestays, toddy shops, waterfalls and jeep operators are cash-only, and ATMs are sparse in the hills. Aim to carry the day's spend + a reserve.

Why not do both hills in fewer days?

Munnar & Wayanad are ~205 km / 8–9 hr apart. The one-way Kochi→Munnar→Wayanad→Kozhikode loop avoids backtracking and keeps both transport cost and travel time low.
